# Evidence Scorecard

Use this reference when a bottleneck thesis needs validation, ranking, or a confidence label.

## 1. Grade The Thesis

Assign one grade before naming companies.

- A: direct company or customer evidence plus cross-chain confirmation
- B: credible company evidence plus industry confirmation, but limited cross-chain proof
- C: plausible industry evidence, but weak company-level confirmation
- D: mostly inference, social signals, or single-source claims

If the grade is C or D, call the output a "direction worth validating", not a confirmed bottleneck.

## 2. Score The Bottleneck

Use 1-5 for each factor.

- demand proof: is the downstream demand visible in orders, guidance, or deployment plans?
- supply concentration: how few credible suppliers exist?
- substitutability: how hard is it to replace this layer without redesign or requalification?
- ramp difficulty: how long does capacity, yield, tooling, or certification take?
- timing: is this bottleneck being felt now, or is it still a future risk?
- consensus gap: is the market already paying for this, or is the layer underfollowed?

Interpretation:

- 24-30: strong bottleneck candidate
- 18-23: plausible, needs more validation
- 12-17: watchlist only
- below 12: weak thesis unless new evidence appears

## 3. Evidence Ledger

For each thesis, keep a short ledger:

- strongest hard evidence
- strongest industry evidence
- cross-chain confirmation
- weakest assumption
- cleanest disproof signal

Do not count repeated versions of the same source as independent confirmation.

## 4. Disproof Signals

Look for evidence that the bottleneck is easing:

- new qualified suppliers
- falling lead times
- management saying capacity is no longer constrained
- price pressure in the bottleneck layer
- downstream customers redesigning around the constraint
- capex additions arriving faster than demand growth

If disproof signals are stronger than proof signals, downgrade the grade even if the narrative sounds attractive.

## 5. Candidate Name Gate

Before giving a watchlist, each name should pass:

- role clarity: the company clearly maps to one layer
- bottleneck exposure: revenue or strategic value is tied to the constrained layer
- evidence quality: at least one hard or credible source supports the role
- timing: there is a reason to monitor it in the user's stated horizon
- risk: the main failure case is explicit

If a company fails role clarity or evidence quality, list it only as "needs validation" or omit it.
