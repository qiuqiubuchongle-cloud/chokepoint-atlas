# Crypto Market Playbook

Use this reference when the user asks for crypto analysis, BTC, ETH, SOL, AI tokens, DePIN tokens, miners, perpetuals, or crypto entry/exit zones.

This workflow is for research and scenario planning. Do not present the result as personalized financial advice, guaranteed signals, or automatic trade instructions.

## 1. Crypto Freshness Gate

Before giving a crypto setup, collect and show:

- asset symbol and venue, such as BTC, ETH, SOL, TAO, RNDR, AKT, or exchange pair
- current price or last traded price
- timestamp and timezone
- 24h range and 24h volume
- BTC and ETH trend context
- spot/perp context if available: funding, open interest, and liquidation zones
- next known catalyst: unlock, upgrade, listing, governance vote, protocol release, major conference, or macro event

If data is stale or unavailable, give only a framework and say what must be refreshed.

## 2. Asset Classes

Classify the crypto exposure before ranking it:

- majors: BTC, ETH, SOL and high-liquidity market beta
- AI / DePIN tokens: assets tied to compute, storage, rendering, model, or data networks
- infrastructure protocols: L1/L2, oracle, restaking, data availability, interoperability
- miners and public crypto equities: equity-like names affected by BTC price, hashprice, power cost, and balance sheet
- high-beta speculative tokens: thin liquidity, narrative-driven, or unlock-heavy assets

Do not mix these groups without stating the different risk model.

## 3. Thesis Checks

For tokens, grade the thesis with crypto-specific evidence:

- protocol traction: usage, fees, TVL, active users, developer activity, or network throughput
- token value capture: whether protocol growth actually benefits the token
- tokenomics: unlock schedule, emissions, FDV, float, insider concentration
- liquidity: exchange depth, volume quality, spread, and availability on major venues
- narrative durability: whether the theme is supported by real usage or only social momentum
- security/governance risk: exploits, centralization, governance capture, bridge risk

If token value capture is unclear, downgrade the thesis even if the project is useful.

## 4. Market Structure Checks

Before giving entry/exit zones, check:

- BTC regime: above/below major moving averages or key support/resistance
- ETH/SOL confirmation if the asset trades as alt beta
- funding: neutral, overheated long, crowded short, or unstable
- open interest: rising with price, rising against price, or washing out
- liquidation map if available
- spot volume versus perp-driven move
- dominance and stablecoin/liquidity context when relevant

Avoid chasing altcoin breakouts when BTC is breaking support or funding is overheated.

## 5. Setup Types

Classify the crypto setup:

- spot-led breakout: preferred when volume and BTC/ETH context confirm
- perp-led squeeze: high risk; needs fast invalidation
- pullback to support: acceptable only if thesis and market regime remain intact
- unlock/event risk: wait or reduce confidence unless price absorbs supply
- range trade: clear support/resistance, no strong catalyst
- no-trade: poor liquidity, unclear invalidation, weak thesis, or crowded funding

## 6. Entry / Add / Exit Zones

Output zones, not single prices:

- starter zone: first risk/reward area
- add zone: confirmation after reclaim, breakout hold, or volume expansion
- no-chase zone: where risk/reward becomes poor or funding is overheated
- invalidation / stop zone: level where the setup is wrong
- first target: resistance or range objective
- stretch target: only if BTC/ETH, funding, volume, and catalyst remain supportive

For thin assets, widen zones and emphasize liquidity risk.

## 7. Crypto Output Template

Use this structure:

1. data timestamp
2. asset universe used
3. BTC/ETH market regime
4. candidate ranking
5. thesis grade and setup grade
6. market-structure read
7. entry/add/no-chase/invalidation/target zones
8. catalysts and unlocks
9. what would make the setup stale

Candidate ranking should include:

- asset
- category
- why it belongs
- evidence quality
- liquidity quality
- setup status: active, watchlist, or no-trade
- main risk

## 8. Crypto Alert Conditions

If the user wants monitoring, define:

- price trigger: reclaim, breakout, pullback, or breakdown level
- funding trigger: overheated long, crowded short, or normalization
- OI trigger: leveraged build-up, squeeze risk, or washout
- catalyst trigger: unlock, upgrade, listing, governance, protocol metric change
- regime trigger: BTC/ETH losing or reclaiming key levels

Each trigger should prompt a research refresh, watchlist move, active setup, trim/reassess, or removal.
